Please apply the following kinds of low-level copyedits throughout the
manuscript:

1. Tighten sentences. Cut filler words and redundant qualifiers. Replace
   phrases like "in order to" with "to", "due to the fact that" with
   "because", "it is important to note that" with nothing.

2. Improve word choice. Replace vague verbs and nouns with concrete ones
   where the intended meaning is clear. Do not change technical terms or
   jargon you are uncertain about.

3. Repair topic sentences. Each paragraph's first sentence should clearly
   preview what the paragraph argues or describes. If the current topic
   sentence buries the paragraph's real claim, rewrite it to lead with
   that claim.

4. Prefer active voice where it does not distort meaning, especially in
   the Discussion. Leave the Methods in passive voice as is conventional.

5. Fix obvious grammar and agreement errors.

6. Preserve the scientific content exactly. Do not alter any numbers,
   statistical claims, citations, figure references, equations, or the
   order of arguments. Do not add or remove citations.
